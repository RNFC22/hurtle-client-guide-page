Your Next Steps

◆

After incorporation, most founders focus on operations — and rightly so. But there are a few digital steps that are easy to delay and painful to leave too long.

01

Secure your business domain name

Register a .com.sg or .sg domain that matches your business name before someone else does. It costs around $20–40/year and forms the foundation of everything digital — your website, your email address, your brand.

Do this this week
02

Set up a professional email address

A Gmail or Hotmail address looks unprofessional when you're sending invoices or pitching clients. Get a domain-based email like hello@yourbusiness.com.sg — Google Workspace starts at $8/month and takes 20 minutes to set up.

Easy win
03

Build your business website

This is the step most new founders delay — and later regret. Your website is the first thing potential clients, investors, and partners check when they hear about your business. Without one, you're invisible to them.

Most important
04

Set up your Google Business Profile

Free, takes 15 minutes, and makes your business show up on Google Maps. Once your website is live, link it here to start building your online presence from day one.

Free to do
05

Pick one social media channel

Don't try to be everywhere at once. Pick the one platform where your customers spend the most time — Instagram for consumer businesses, LinkedIn for B2B — and focus on that. Your website is home base. Social media sends people there.

When you're ready

What Happens When Your Business Has No Website

◆

Most founders know they need a website. Here's what's actually at stake when it keeps getting pushed back.

🔍 Search

Customers can't find you

When someone hears about your business and Googles it, nothing comes up. Many won't take the next step — they'll move on to a competitor who does have a website.

🤝 Trust

You look less credible

In Singapore's business culture, having no website raises an eyebrow. It signals that the business is either very new, not serious, or not yet ready to trade professionally.

📊 Leads

Enquiries go to competitors

People searching for your type of service online will find someone else. Every week without a website is a week of potential leads going elsewhere.

✦ Brand

Your brand story goes untold

A website is where you explain who you are, what you do, and why someone should choose you — in your own words, not squeezed into an Instagram bio.
